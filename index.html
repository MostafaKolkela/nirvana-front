Understanding IFTA

What is IFTA?

The International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) is an agreement among the 48 contiguous U.S. states and Canadian provinces to simplify fuel tax reporting for motor carriers operating across multiple jurisdictions.


Trucking companies must file an IFTA report quarterly, detailing total miles traveled in each state and all fuel purchased — including date, seller, gallons, and price.


United Fuel Advantage

Our cards automatically generate itemized fuel statements sorted by date, location, and jurisdiction — making IFTA filing fast and accurate.

Starting Out
CDL Requirements
🎂

Age Requirement

At least 18 years old for intrastate driving; 21+ for interstate (cross-state) commerce.

📋

CDL License

Commercial Driver's License required. Issued by your state's DMV after written and driving tests.

🏥

Medical Exam

DOT physical examination required. Must meet vision, hearing, and health standards set by FMCSA.

🔍

Background Check

Clean driving and criminal background record required. Motor Vehicle Record (MVR) is reviewed.

🧪

Drug Testing

Pre-employment drug and alcohol testing is mandatory under DOT/FMCSA regulations.

🏢

Start a Carrier

To run your own trucking company you'll need: EIN, DOT & MC numbers, insurance, IFTA registration, and FMCSA authority.
